Top 3 Must-Visits in Pakistan

  • Go to Faisal Mosque

  • Go to Badshahi Mosque

  • Go to Saif-Ul-Malook

Faisal Mosque

The Faisal Mosque is the largest mosque in Pakistan, located in the national capital city of Islamabad. Completed in 1986, it was designed by Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay, shaped like a desert Bedouin's tent, is an iconic symbol of Islamabad throughout the world.

Fun Fact

Faisal Mosque was a deliberate departure from traditional mosque architecture.

Badshahi Mosque

The Badshahi Mosque in Lahore was commissioned by the sixth Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. Constructed between 1671 and 1673, it was the largest mosque in the world upon construction. It is the second largest mosque in Pakistan and the fifth largest mosque in the world.

Fun Fact

its courtyard is so large, the entire main platform of the Taj Mahal could fit inside it.

Saif-Ul-Malook

Lake Saif ul Malook is famous for its stunning natural beauty.The lake is a high-altitude alpine lake in the Kaghan Valley of Pakistan, known for its crystal-clear, turquoise waters and surrounding snow-capped peaks.

Fun Fact

It's called the lake of fairies because of a local tale about a prince and a princess.
